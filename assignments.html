Capstone Project< Capstone Project Small businesses that are handing New York State resident data are expected to comply with the Stop Hacks and Improve Electronic Data Security Act or the SHIELD Act. Although they are expected to comply with this act there is limited research that is available to show how they are achieving these requirements. The purpose of this proposed study is to determine how small businesses approach compliance with the SHILED Act. This study focuses on awareness, implementation methods, resource constraints, and internal or external IT support. These findings can be achieve though the quantitative survey that is proposed to collect this information to determine how small businesses are complying with the SHIELD Act. Since this study is conceptual, expected results are calculated by existing literature and industry reports. These are used to determine the expected results of small businesses filling out the survey. While offering insight to inform both small businesses and policy makers and future research based on this area.
Ethical Hacking Ethical Hacking and Network Defense This paper is a deep drive into four different laws and regulations that can affect ethical hackers. The three that are covered are Computer Fraud and Abuse Act of 1984, New York Penal Law 156.00 to 156.50, ND Century Code 12.1-06.1-08, and CA penal code 502. Ethical Hackers should be aware of laws and regulation such as these to ensure they are protected when conducting secuirty tests on a network. Without understanding of state and federal laws, ethical hackers can risk the potential of legal actions taken against them.
